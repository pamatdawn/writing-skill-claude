# E-E-A-T Framework — Content Integration Guide

**Source:** Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines  
**Purpose:** Embed Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness into every article to satisfy both readers and Google's quality signals.

---

## What Is E-E-A-T?

E-E-A-T stands for **Experience · Expertise · Authoritativeness · Trustworthiness**. Google's Search Quality Raters use this framework to assess content quality. High E-E-A-T content ranks better and converts better.

> **Note:** The "double E" (Experience) was added in December 2022. First-hand experience now matters alongside book knowledge.

---

## Pillar 1 — Experience (the first E)

**Definition:** The content creator has real, first-hand experience with the topic.

### Signals to include:
- Personal anecdotes: "When I tested this method on a client site…"
- Specific numbers from real use: "After 90 days, organic traffic increased 34%."
- Lessons learned, including failures: "What we got wrong the first time was…"
- Photos, screenshots, case study data (even described in text).

### Implementation pattern:
```
[EXPERIENCE BLOCK]
Add a 2–3 sentence paragraph per major H2 that grounds the advice
in a real scenario. Use first-person where natural. If writing on 
behalf of a brand, attribute experience to "our team" or cite a 
named contributor.
```

### Checklist:
- [ ] At least one first-hand data point or scenario per article
- [ ] Specific, verifiable details (not vague generalizations)
- [ ] "Lessons learned" or cautionary note where relevant

---

## Pillar 2 — Expertise

**Definition:** The author has deep knowledge of the subject — formal credentials, years of practice, or demonstrated mastery.

### Signals to include:
- Author bio with credentials (job title, years of experience, certifications).
- Accurate use of technical terminology — defined on first use.
- Step-by-step processes that only a practitioner would know.
- Citations to primary research (journals, official documentation, .gov/.edu sources).
- Acknowledgment of nuance and edge cases.

### Implementation pattern:
```
[EXPERTISE SIGNALS]
- Open intro with a bold claim + supporting statistic from a credible source.
- Use precise technical vocabulary; define abbreviations.
- Avoid hedging language ("might", "could possibly") when facts are established.
- Include at least one "deep-dive" section that goes beyond surface-level advice.
```

### Checklist:
- [ ] Author credentials mentioned (in bio or article intro)
- [ ] At least 1 stat or data point from a primary source
- [ ] Technical terms defined on first use
- [ ] No factual inaccuracies or outdated information
- [ ] Edge cases or limitations acknowledged

---

## Pillar 3 — Authoritativeness

**Definition:** The site and author are recognized as go-to sources by other authoritative entities.

### Signals to include:
- Outbound links to .gov, .edu, or top-tier publications.
- Mentions of or quotes from recognized industry experts.
- Internal links to other depth content on the same site (topical authority).
- Publishing date visible and content updated regularly.
- Byline with a real, searchable author name.

### Implementation pattern:
```
[AUTHORITY SIGNALS]
- Cite at least one external authoritative source per article.
- Link internally to at least 2 related articles (topical cluster).
- Include "Last Updated: [Date]" near the top for evergreen content.
- Quote or reference an industry leader if relevant.
```

### Checklist:
- [ ] 1–2 outbound links to authoritative sources (with `rel="nofollow"` if needed)
- [ ] 2+ internal links to related content
- [ ] Author name is a real, bylined person
- [ ] Publish/update date visible

---

## Pillar 4 — Trustworthiness

**Definition:** The content, site, and author are honest, transparent, and safe to use.

### Signals to include:
- Clear disclosure of affiliate links, sponsored content, or conflicts of interest.
- Accurate, fact-checked information — no misleading headlines.
- Privacy policy and contact information accessible from the page.
- HTTPS (secure site).
- Balanced perspective — acknowledges opposing viewpoints where relevant.
- Sources cited in-line or in a references section.

### Implementation pattern:
```
[TRUST SIGNALS]
- Add a disclaimer if article covers medical, legal, or financial topics:
  "This article is for informational purposes only and does not 
   constitute [medical/legal/financial] advice."
- Cite all statistics inline: (Source: [Name], [Year]).
- Avoid clickbait titles that overpromise.
- If recommending a product/service, disclose any relationship.
```

### Checklist:
- [ ] No misleading or exaggerated claims
- [ ] Relevant disclaimer added (YMYL topics)
- [ ] All statistics cited with source + year
- [ ] Affiliate/sponsored disclosure if applicable
- [ ] Balanced viewpoint — acknowledges limitations

## E-E-A-T by Topic Category (YMYL Priority)

**YMYL** (Your Money or Your Life) topics require the highest E-E-A-T standard:

| Category | Extra Requirements |
|----------|-------------------|
| Health / Medical | MD or healthcare professional byline OR review by one |
| Finance / Legal | Licensed professional byline or expert review note |
| Safety / News | Primary sources only; no speculation |
| General / Lifestyle | Standard E-E-A-T sufficient |

## Quick E-E-A-T Score Card

Rate each pillar 1–3 before publishing:

| Pillar | 1 — Weak | 2 — Adequate | 3 — Strong |
|--------|----------|--------------|------------|
| Experience | Generic advice, no real examples | 1 personal example | Multiple specific, verifiable examples |
| Expertise | No credentials, vague advice | Credentials mentioned | Deep technical content + primary sources |
| Authoritativeness | No links, no byline | Byline + 1 source | Named author + multiple sources + internal links |
| Trustworthiness | Unverified claims, no disclosure | Some citations | Full citations + disclaimer + balanced view |

**Minimum to publish:** All pillars at score 2 or above.  
**Target for competitive keywords:** All pillars at score 3.

## Template Snippets

### Author Bio Block (paste at article end)
```
**About the Author**  
[Name] is a [Title] with [X] years of experience in [field]. 
[He/She/They] has [credential or notable achievement]. 
Connect on [LinkedIn URL].
```

### Disclaimer Block (YMYL topics)
```
**Disclaimer:** This content is for informational purposes only 
and does not constitute professional [medical/legal/financial] advice. 
Always consult a qualified professional before making decisions 
based on this information.
```

### In-line Citation Format
```
According to [Source Name] ([Year]), [stat or claim]. ([Source URL])
```
