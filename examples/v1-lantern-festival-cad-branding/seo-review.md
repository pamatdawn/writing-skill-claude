### Additional content-quality observations
- **Word count:** ~720 words of body copy. Below the 1,500-word blog floor in the seo-content skill. For a high-intent, seasonal commercial query ("2026 Lantern Festival Thailand"), this is thin.
- **Readability:** Sentence length is reasonable (avg ~16–20 words). Paragraphs are short. Vocabulary is appropriate for a general traveler audience. No obvious AI-generic phrasing, though "magical spectacle that enchants both local residents and international visitors" is the kind of generic travel-blog line Google's QRG flags.
- **Keyword usage:** Primary keyword "Lantern Festival" appears in H1, intro, and headings — natural density. However, the article fragments its keyword target by mixing "Lantern Festival", "CAD Lantern Festival", "Yi Peng", and "Loy Krathong" without a clear definition section that tells Google these are the same event family.
- **Heading hierarchy:** Clean H1 -> H2 -> H3. Numbered TOC matches H2s. Good.
- **Internal/external linking:** Zero links in the body. Major miss for both SEO and conversion.
- **Multimedia:** No images, alt text, video, or infographic referenced in the doc.
- **AI-content markers:** Two tables (venue comparison, dates) and clean lists are positive. The "Venue Comparison Table" rates the in-town option as "Somewhat disorganized" with audience "Locals/Backpackers" — this is opinion stated as fact and is the kind of unsupported judgement that fails QRG "accurate, well-sourced claims".
- **Audience mismatch flag:** Article is in English but the agency's audience is Portuguese/Spanish speakers. The comparison table labels the CAD audience as "International/Brazilians", which is awkward in English copy and suggests the doc was translated from PT/ES without localisation review.

## Top 5 highest-impact fixes

1. **Replace "CAD Lantern Festival" branding with the real entity names "Yi Peng" and "Loy Krathong" throughout.** This is the single most damaging issue: the article is currently optimising for a brand name with zero global search volume while ignoring the two terms travelers actually type. Keep "Chiang Mai Arts & Design (CAD)" as a single, sourced reference for the ticketed event organiser, but rename the H1, TOC, and date table to use "Yi Peng / Loy Krathong" or "Thailand Lantern Festival (Yi Peng)".

2. **Add a 134–167 word "What is the Yi Peng Lantern Festival?" definition block as the first content after the H1.** Currently the article opens with promotional language ("dazzling cultural celebrations in Southeast Asia") instead of an extractable definition. AI engines need a self-contained answer in the first 60 words to cite the page.

3. **Add author byline + Mundo Asia Tours organisation block with credentials, dates, and outbound citations.** Specifically: named consultant author (with role and years of Southeast Asia experience), "Published: YYYY-MM-DD / Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD" visible in the body, link to Tourism Authority of Thailand's official Yi Peng page, link to CAD's official event page, and 2–3 named past-edition references (e.g., a news article covering the 2025 edition). Currently zero outbound links exist.

4. **Add original multimedia: at least one hero image and one infographic.** The topic is a visual spectacle and the article has zero images. Add (a) a high-quality original photo of a past Mundo Asia Tours group at the festival (Experience signal), (b) an infographic version of the venue-comparison table, (c) a simple map of Chiang Mai showing CAD vs Doi Saket vs Old City. 5. **Expand depth from ~720 to 1,500+ words by adding three commercial-intent FAQ sections** with question-form H3s: "How much does the Yi Peng Lantern Festival cost in 2026?", "Is the Lantern Festival in Chiang Mai worth it?", and "Can you release sky lanterns in Bangkok?". ## Concrete rewrites

### Rewrite 1 — Opening paragraph (currently weakest for citability)

**Original (verbatim, 39 words):**
> "The Lantern Festival in Thailand is one of the most dazzling cultural celebrations in Southeast Asia. As thousands of lanterns illuminate the sky and float gently upon the waters, the festival creates a magical spectacle that enchants both local residents and international visitors."

**Problems:** No definition, no entity name, no date, no location anchor, no statistic, opens with subjective adjectives ("dazzling", "magical") that AI engines will not cite.

**Rewritten (147 words, hits the 134–167 citability window):**
> The Thailand Lantern Festival is the popular name for two overlapping celebrations held each November in Chiang Mai: **Yi Peng**, when paper sky lanterns (khom loi) are released into the night, and **Loy Krathong**, when small floating offerings (krathongs) are placed on rivers and ponds. Both follow the Thai lunar calendar and fall on the full moon of the 12th lunar month. In 2026, the main festival dates are **24–25 November**, with the largest organised lantern release on 25 November at the Chiang Mai Arts & Design (CAD) Heritage venue, about 45 minutes outside the city. Travelers from Brazil, Portugal, Spain, and across South America typically combine the festival with a Bangkok arrival, a Chiang Mai stay of 3–4 nights, and an optional beach extension to Phuket or Krabi. Tickets to the organised release sell out roughly four to five months in advance.

### Rewrite 2 — Section 2 heading and intro

**Original heading (verbatim):**
> "When is the CAD Lantern Festival in 2025, 2026, and beyond?"

**Problems:** "CAD Lantern Festival" is not a term anyone searches for. The heading also drops 2025 (now in the past for a 2026-13 reader) which makes the page look stale.

**Rewritten heading:**
> "When is the Yi Peng Lantern Festival in Thailand? Dates from 2026 to 2030"

**Add an intro paragraph above the table (110 words):**
> Yi Peng and Loy Krathong fall on the full moon of the 12th month of the Thai lunar calendar, which usually lands in November. Because the Thai lunar calendar drifts relative to the Gregorian calendar, the exact date moves each year. The Tourism Authority of Thailand publishes confirmed dates roughly nine to twelve months in advance, and the Chiang Mai Arts & Design (CAD) organised lantern release announces its own dates shortly after. Years marked "provisional" reflect lunar calendar projections and may shift by one or two days once Thai authorities confirm them.
