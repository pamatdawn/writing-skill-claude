## Concrete rewrites

### Rewrite 1 — TAT claim (currently weakest for authority)

**Original (verbatim, 29 words):**
> "According to the Tourism Authority of Thailand, Chiang Mai's combined Yi Peng / Loy Krathong programming draws hundreds of thousands of visitors each year."

**Problems:** No year, no report title, no URL, no specific figure. This is the kind of vague authority claim the seo-content skill flags as failing "accurate, well-sourced claims". A TAT footer reference alone is not a citation.

**Rewritten (138 words, hits the 134–167 citability window):**
> The Tourism Authority of Thailand reports that the combined Yi Peng / Loy Krathong programming in Chiang Mai is one of the largest cultural events in the kingdom each November. Recent editions have drawn an estimated 300,000+ domestic and international visitors across the two festival nights, according to TAT's annual northern-region tourism brief [link to specific TAT brief, year, and page]. International arrivals concentrate around the CAD Heritage release, the Doi Saket release, and the free temple and moat releases inside the Old City. Mundo Asia Tours' operations team has observed peak crowd density between 7:30 PM and 9:30 PM on both festival nights, with the highest international share on Night 2. *Note to writer: replace the bracketed citation with the actual TAT brief title, year, and URL — do not invent figures.*

### Rewrite 2 — FAQ "Is Yi Peng family-friendly?" (currently weakest for citability)

**Original (verbatim, 21 words including question):**
> "Is Yi Peng family-friendly? Yes. Children are welcome at all ticketed events. Mundo Asia Tours offers family-rate packages with shared lanterns."

**Problems:** 21 words is well below the 134–167 word citability target. AI engines cannot extract a meaningful answer. No age guidance, no stroller/accessibility note, no comment on noise or crowd density. The FAQ format also uses bold-and-inline rather than an H3 + answer block, which weakens FAQPage schema readiness.

**Rewritten as an H3 + answer (144 words):**
> ### Is the Yi Peng Festival family-friendly?
> Yes. Children of all ages are welcome at the ticketed events (CAD Heritage, Doi Saket) and at the free in-town releases along the Chiang Mai moat. Premium ticketed events are stroller-accessible; the free temple releases at Wat Phan Tao and Wat Lok Moli are more crowded and uneven underfoot, so we recommend a baby carrier rather than a stroller for children under three. Lantern release itself is a two-person task — one adult holds the lantern frame while a child can light the fuel cell under supervision. Crowd density peaks between 7:30 PM and 9:30 PM on Night 2; families with young children may prefer Night 1 or the Doi Saket venue, where the crowds are smaller. Mundo Asia Tours offers family-rate packages with shared lanterns and a dedicated bilingual guide for groups travelling with children under twelve.
