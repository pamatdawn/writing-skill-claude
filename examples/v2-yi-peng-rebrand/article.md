On the night of the twelfth full moon of the Lanna lunar calendar, tens of thousands of paper lanterns rise above Chiang Mai while smaller *krathong* boats drift down the Ping River for the parallel **Loy Krathong** festival. ## 1. What Is the Yi Peng Festival? Yi Peng vs. Loy Krathong

**Yi Peng is a Lanna (northern Thai) Buddhist festival of light.** Releasing a *khom loi* sky lantern is an act of letting go—of misfortune, regret, or attachment—and an offering of merit for the year ahead. The festival is rooted in the eight-hundred-year-old Lanna Kingdom that once ruled the highlands around Chiang Mai, and the rituals are tied to the lunar calendar rather than a fixed Gregorian date. [INTERNAL LINK: Lanna culture history]

Yi Peng overlaps with **Loy Krathong**, the nationwide Thai festival held on the same full-moon night. The two are often confused; the distinction is simple:

- **Yi Peng** *(sky)*: releasing lanterns into the air to symbolize letting go of negativity and praying for renewal. Practiced primarily in the Lanna north—Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, Mae Hong Son.
- **Loy Krathong** *(water)*: floating small lotus-shaped boats of banana leaf and flowers down a river to thank the Water Goddess, **Phra Mae Khongkha**, for the year's water. Practiced across all of Thailand.

In Chiang Mai, the two run on the same nights, which is why most travelers experience both back-to-back. According to the Tourism Authority of Thailand, Chiang Mai's combined Yi Peng / Loy Krathong programming draws hundreds of thousands of visitors each year. ## 3. Three Ways to Experience Yi Peng in Chiang Mai

There is no single "official" Yi Peng venue. Travelers choose from three very different experiences, each with its own price, scale, and atmosphere.

**CAD Heritage** is the premium ticketed event organized by **Chiang Mai Arts & Design (CAD)**, held outside town in a heritage-themed venue. The simultaneous release of thousands of lanterns at a single signal is what produces the iconic "sky full of light" photos you have seen on social media. Tickets typically sell out four to five months in advance, especially the VIP and Elite tiers.

**Doi Saket** is the mid-tier ticketed alternative held in a quieter rural setting about 30 minutes from the Old City. It delivers the same simultaneous-release experience at a lower price point and with smaller crowds.

**In-town public release** happens organically along the moat and at temples such as Wat Phan Tao and Wat Lok Moli in Chiang Mai's Old City. There is no entry fee. The trade-off is that releases are scattered through the evening rather than choreographed, and Chiang Mai's local government may restrict lantern release at certain temple sites for safety reasons—always check current municipal announcements before you go.

## 4. Day-of Schedule: What a Premium Yi Peng Ticket Looks Like

Based on the schedules our team has run for Mundo Asia Tours clients across the 2022, 2023, and 2024 editions, here is the typical timing for a premium CAD Heritage night:

- **1:00 PM — Meeting at departure point.** Shuttle buses collect guests from designated hotels. Arrive early; Chiang Mai traffic on festival night is heavy.
- **4:00 PM — Entry and parallel activities.** Ticket check, then a 90-minute window to explore Lanna dance performances, handicraft workshops, lantern-decorating stations, and photo zones.
- **6:00 PM — Dinner service.** A traditional northern Thai (Khantoke-style) buffet. VIP and Elite ticket holders dine in separate seating with assigned tables.
- **7:30 – 8:00 PM — Official opening ceremony.** Cultural performances, then a 20-minute Buddhist chanting led by monks, followed by a 10-minute group meditation. This is the cultural anchor of the night and is often skipped by photographers—do not skip it.
- **8:30 – 9:00 PM — Lantern release and floating krathongs.** The main event. On a single signal, every guest releases a lantern simultaneously. A *krathong* float on the venue's water feature follows.

Most premium events conclude by 10:00 PM, with shuttle buses returning guests to their hotels by 11:30 PM.

## 5. How to Plan Your Yi Peng Festival Trip from South America

Yi Peng is a high-demand window, and Chiang Mai's hotel inventory near the Old City is limited. Three planning rules our consultants give every Mundo Asia client:

1. **Book flights to Bangkok first, Chiang Mai second.** Direct flights from São Paulo or Buenos Aires to Bangkok (via Doha, Dubai, or Istanbul) are easier to lock in than the Bangkok → Chiang Mai domestic leg. Book the domestic flight or overnight train (the State Railway of Thailand "Special Express" sleeper) immediately after.
2. **Stay in the Old City or Nimmanhaemin ("Nimman").** The Old City puts you walking distance from public Loy Krathong releases at the moat and major temples. Nimman is the modern café district to the west, popular with younger travelers. Avoid hotels in the Hang Dong or San Sai districts unless you have a shuttle—they are 40+ minutes from the main events.
3. **Book four to six months in advance.** This is the only way to lock in fair airfare and your choice of ticket tier. By August of festival year, premium CAD Heritage tickets are typically sold out.

## 6. Yi Peng Safety, Etiquette, and Sustainability

Lantern releases are beautiful and *consequential*. Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX) closes briefly during the mass release windows to avoid lantern-aircraft conflicts—respect designated release zones for this reason.

- **Fire safety.** Only release lanterns in officially designated zones. The bamboo frame and waxed-fuel cell are real fire hazards in residential areas.
- **Dress code.** Cover shoulders and knees, especially at temple grounds. Yi Peng is a religious event, not a music festival.
- **Sustainability.** Buy biodegradable lanterns (bamboo + rice paper, no wire frames) and biodegradable *krathongs* (banana leaf, bread, or ice—never polystyrene). The Chiang Mai municipality fishes thousands of krathongs out of the Ping River the next morning; biodegradable options ease the cleanup.
- **Scam protection.** Counterfeit tickets are common on social media in the weeks before the event. Buy through CAD's official channels or a licensed agency. Mundo Asia Tours holds direct allocation from CAD and can confirm ticket authenticity in writing.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Yi Peng Festival the same as the Loy Krathong Festival?**
No. They are two different festivals that happen on the same nights. Yi Peng is a Lanna northern-Thai festival of sky lanterns; Loy Krathong is the nationwide Thai festival of floating water offerings. In Chiang Mai they merge into a single celebration.

**Can I release a lantern anywhere in Chiang Mai?**
No. Chiang Mai municipality designates specific release zones for safety reasons, and the airport coordinates brief closures around the mass-release windows. Always release in approved areas.

**Do I need to buy a ticket to see Yi Peng?**
No. The free, in-town releases at the moat and at certain temples are open to everyone. Ticketed events (CAD Heritage, Doi Saket) buy you a *simultaneous* mass-release moment with thousands of other guests, plus dinner, transport, and cultural programming.

**When do CAD Heritage tickets go on sale for 2026?**
Allocations typically open in May or June of the festival year. By August, premium tiers are usually gone. Book by September at the latest for any tier.
